Resources  ·  small collection

Things I read,
use, and quietly recommend.

A working list of the tools, books, and people that have shaped how I think about this work. Updated when something earns it.

i. Tools
The short list — things I'd genuinely have a worse day without.
  • Linear project management
    The only PM tool that respects keyboard users. Pricing got worse, value still high.
    linear.app
  • Cursor code editor
    Where most of my code now happens. The AI is genuinely useful; the UI doesn't get in the way.
    cursor.com
  • Figma design
    Still no real competitor. Even the dev mode has earned its place.
    figma.com
  • Velite content layer
    Type-safe MDX without the Contentlayer drama. Powers this site.
    velite.js.org
ii. Books
Books I return to, not the ones I finished first.
  • White  by Kenya Hara
    The clearest argument for restraint I've ever read. Read it slowly; it's 100 pages and you'll need them.
    muji books
  • Grid Systems  by Müller-Brockmann
    The bible. Skip the rhetoric, study the diagrams.
    niggli press
  • Refactoring UI  by Adam Wathan & Steve Schoger
    Practical, mid-career, no-nonsense. Recommended for engineers who design.
    refactoringui.com
  • A Pattern Language  by Christopher Alexander
    About buildings, but about everything. Architects, designers, and town planners pay attention.
    oxford press
iii. Essays
Bookmarked, re-read, and recommended at dinner parties.
  • Worse Is Better  by Richard P. Gabriel
    The original case for shipping. Still painful, still right.
    dreamsongs.com
  • Why Generative Art Is Hard  by Tyler Hobbs
    A working artist's honest accounting of why this looks easy and isn't.
    tylerxhobbs.com
  • The Pixar Theory of Animation  by Glen Keane
    Best 12 minutes on motion design I've found.
    vimeo
iv. People
Whose work I quietly study. Most are still working.
  • Rauno Freiberg  designer, Vercel
    For the experimental hero, the precise interaction, the designer-to-designer wink.
    rauno.me
  • Massimo Vignelli  1931 — 2014
    The voice in my head when I'm being lazy about the grid.
    vignelli.com
  • Kenya Hara  art director, MUJI
    The case study for restraint as a design philosophy, not a lack of effort.
    ndc.co.jp
