About · A few words

I design and build
small, careful things.

Hello. I'm Asher — a designer and engineer based in Shanghai. I've spent the last decade quietly making things for the web; this is where I keep notes about what I'm learning.

A longer note

I came to this work through writing first. Long before I knew what kerning meant or could spell TypeScript, I was obsessing over which paragraph break felt right. The instincts stayed; the medium just shifted.

These days I split my time between client engagements (mostly design systems and small product surfaces) and self-initiated work that lives here. I prefer projects where I can do both the design and the engineering — the seam between the two is where the interesting decisions hide.

If you're here looking for a collaborator, I'm occasionally taking on new work. The contact page has the details.

What I care about

Four small dogmas
  • Honest placeholders beat pretty fictions. A grey block with a label is more useful than a fake chart.
  • One detail at 120%, everything else at 80%. Taste is knowing where to spend the effort.
  • Static beats dynamic, until proven otherwise. Most pages do not need to be interactive.
  • Done is a feature. Ship the small thing.
