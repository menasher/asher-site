I came to this work through writing first. Long before I knew what kerning meant or could spell TypeScript, I was obsessing over which paragraph break felt right. The instincts stayed; the medium just shifted.

These days I split my time between client engagements (mostly design systems and small product surfaces) and self-initiated work that lives here. I prefer projects where I can do both the design and the engineering — the seam between the two is where the interesting decisions hide.

What I care about — small dogmas

01.

Honest placeholders.

A grey block with a label is more useful than a fake chart. Pretend less.
02.

One detail at 120%.

Everything else at 80%. Taste is knowing where to spend the effort.
03.

Static beats dynamic.

Until proven otherwise. Most pages don't need to be interactive.
04.

Done is a feature.

Ship the small thing. Then ship the next one.
