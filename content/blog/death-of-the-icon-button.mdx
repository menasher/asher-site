## The pattern

A square. An icon. No label. You hover and *maybe* a tooltip appears after
600ms. You tap on mobile and the tooltip never appears because there is no
hover state.

This is the most overused control in modern UI. We use it because it&apos;s
visually quiet — and that&apos;s exactly why it&apos;s misused.

## What it costs

- **Accessibility**: screen readers fall back to `aria-label`, which is
  rarely good
- **Mobile**: no hover, no tooltip, no label, no learnability
- **Localisation**: an icon means nothing if your audience doesn&apos;t share
  the visual idiom

## When it earns its place

- The icon is overwhelmingly conventional (✕ for close, 🔍 for search, ⚙ for settings)
- The control appears in a context that already explains it (close on a modal)
- There&apos;s a visible label nearby that pairs with it

## What I do instead

Most of the time? **Label + icon**, like every text-heavy interface from
1995. Put the verb on the button. Save the icon-only treatment for the
five conventional cases.

There&apos;s nothing brave about being illegible.
